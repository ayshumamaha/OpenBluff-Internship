Structural Finite Element Analysis and Design Optimization of a Load-Bearing L-Bracket Using ANSYS Mechanical
Mechanical Design & Simulation Internship – OpenBluff
📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of a structural steel L-bracket subjected to static loading using ANSYS Mechanical. The primary objective was to evaluate the structural performance of the bracket under a constant external load, identify critical stress concentration regions, and implement a design optimization to improve its structural integrity.

The analysis included geometry modelling, material selection, boundary condition definition, mesh convergence, static structural simulation, result interpretation, and validation of the optimized design. The modified geometry successfully exceeded the internship requirement by improving the Factor of Safety by more than 30% while maintaining the original functional dimensions.

🎯 Objectives
Develop a 3D CAD model of a load-bearing L-bracket.
Perform static structural analysis using ANSYS Mechanical.
Evaluate stress, deformation, strain, and safety factor.
Identify critical stress concentration regions.
Optimize the bracket geometry to improve structural performance.
Validate the optimized design through comparative FEA.

Project Outcome

The optimized L-bracket demonstrated a significant improvement in structural performance under identical loading conditions. By increasing the plate thickness and enlarging the internal fillet radius, the maximum equivalent (von Mises) stress was reduced from 28.194 MPa to 15.369 MPa, corresponding to a 45.49% reduction. Consequently, the calculated Factor of Safety increased from 8.87 to 16.27, representing an 83.43% improvement, which significantly exceeds the internship requirement of a minimum 30% increase in structural safety.
